Description

Steak is a popular and versatile dish that centers around a cut of beef, typically grilled or pan-seared to achieve a flavorful, caramelized crust while maintaining a juicy interior. The key to a perfect steak lies in the selection of quality meat, proper seasoning, and accurate cooking to the desired level of doneness. It's a favorite in many cuisines, often served with sides like vegetables, potatoes, or salads.

Ingredients

  • Quality steak cut (such as ribeye, sirloin, or filet mignon)
  • Salt and pepper
  • Cooking oil (with high smoke point, like canola or grapeseed)
  • Butter
  • Optional: garlic, herbs (like thyme or rosemary)

Steps

  1. Preparation: Season the steak generously with salt and pepper.
  2. Cooking: Heat oil in a pan over high heat. Sear steak on both sides until browned.
  3. Flavoring: Add butter, garlic, and herbs to the pan, basting the steak.
  4. Resting: Let the steak rest for a few minutes after cooking for juciness.
  5. Serving: Slice against the grain and serve.
